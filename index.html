To Save The World

AI

Artificial Intelligence has great potential to save our planet: detecting energy emission reductions, optimizing energy production, monitoring deforestation, predicting extreme weather conditions, cleaning oceans and protecting inhabitants, and much more.

In 2020 researchers from the University of Buffalo developed a robotic system based on machine learning that allows sorting plastics and discovering ecologically friendly solvents and new chemicals that decompose plastics into reusable components.

This AI system can help our environment tremoundously if used correctly which is exactly why, AI is one of the most promising solutions to problems that we experience everyday.

Dyson Sphere

Our current annual global energy consumption is estimated to be 580 million terajoules –– roughly equivalent to the energy we would use if all 7.5 billion of us boiled 70 kettles of water per hour every day for a year. 80% of this energy comes from fossil fuels, a leading source of global warming pollution. A solution to this would be a theoretical way to harness the sun - via a dyson sphere.

A Dyson Sphere is a ring, or multiple rings of super high power solar panels that would orbit around the sun and absorb all the energy from it - effectively giving us unlimited energy.

Plant Based Plastic

Plant-based plastics offer an alternative to fossil-based plastics, with a lot of the same capabilities. Sourcing from renewable plant-based materials instead of a finite source of petroleum is great for the environment and helps slow climate-change.

Plant-based plastics and materials reduce our reliance on petroleum, and will guarantee a solution for plastic pollution.

Mars Base

Exploring Mars could potentially save the human species if Earth should undergo planetary extinction. Over the last century, everything we have learned about Mars suggests that the planet was once quite capable of hosting ecosystems—and that it might still be an incubator for microbial life today. This mean if Earth has gone to a state of zero recovery, we can still survive on Mars

Nuclear Fusion

Fusion promises a virtually limitless form of energy that, unlike fossil fuels, emits zero greenhouse gases and, unlike the nuclear fission power used today, produces no long-life radioactive waste.

Mastering it could literally save humanity from climate change, a crisis of our own making.
